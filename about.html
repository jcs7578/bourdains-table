What Is This?

Eat Like Anthony is an interactive map of every restaurant, street stall, market, and hole-in-the-wall that Anthony Bourdain visited across his four television shows. It's a tribute to the man who showed us that the best meals are rarely found in the fanciest places — and that food is the most direct route into a culture's heart.

The dataset includes 133 locations across 46 countries, from Katz's Deli in Manhattan to a longhouse in the Borneo jungle. Each entry includes what he ate, which show featured it, and what made the visit memorable.

The Shows

A Cook's Tour

Food Network · 2002–2003 · 2 Seasons, 35 Episodes

Where it all began. Fresh off the success of Kitchen Confidential, Bourdain set out to eat the world. From the back alleys of Vietnam to the markets of Tokyo, this show established the blueprint: no scripts, no pretension, just a curious chef with a plane ticket and an empty stomach. Vietnam changed him forever — he said he fell in love with the country on this trip and never recovered.

No Reservations

Travel Channel · 2005–2012 · 9 Seasons, 142 Episodes

The show that made Bourdain a household name. Sharper, more ambitious, and unafraid to go dark when the story demanded it. The Beirut episode — interrupted by the 2006 war — became one of the most acclaimed hours of travel television ever made. No Reservations proved food television could be journalism, memoir, and adventure all at once.

The Layover

Travel Channel · 2011–2013 · 2 Seasons, 20 Episodes

A tighter format: what would Bourdain do with just 24–48 hours in a city? The Layover stripped away the narrative ambition and focused purely on eating and drinking — a rapid-fire guide to the essential spots. Hong Kong, Singapore, Montreal, Rome, Los Angeles — each episode was a masterclass in knowing exactly where to go and what to order.

Parts Unknown

CNN · 2013–2018 · 12 Seasons, 96 Episodes

The masterwork. Parts Unknown was less a food show than a document of the human condition, told through meals. Bourdain went to Iran, Congo, Myanmar, Libya, and the South Bronx. He dined with Obama in Hanoi on plastic stools. He showed us that sitting down to eat with someone is the most powerful thing you can do to understand them. Five Peabody Awards. The greatest travel show ever made.

How to Use the Map

  • Explore: Pan and zoom the world map. Click any marker to see what Bourdain ate there.
  • Find nearby spots: Enter your US zip code or click "Use My Location" to sort every restaurant by distance from you.
  • Filter by show: Click the stamp-style buttons to show only restaurants from a specific series.
  • Search: Type a restaurant name, city, or dish to narrow the directory.
  • Click a card: The map flies to that location and opens the popup.
  • Click a marker: The sidebar scrolls to and highlights the matching entry.

Anthony Bourdain (1956–2018)

Anthony Michael Bourdain was an American chef, author, and television personality. He started as a line cook in New York City, eventually becoming executive chef at Brasserie Les Halles. His 2000 book Kitchen Confidential: Adventures in the Culinary Underbelly became a bestseller and launched his career as television's most compelling food storyteller.

Over nearly two decades of television, Bourdain visited more than 80 countries. He championed street food vendors and hole-in-the-wall joints alongside Michelin-starred restaurants. He was an advocate for immigrant cooks, underrepresented cuisines, and the idea that the best way to understand a place is to sit down and eat with its people.

"If I'm an advocate for anything, it's to move. As far as you can, as much as you can. Across the ocean, or simply across the river. Walk in someone else's shoes or at least eat their food."

Data Sources

Restaurant data was compiled and cross-referenced from:

  • Anthony Bourdain World Map
  • Eat Like Bourdain
  • No Reservations Locations
  • Wikipedia episode guides for each show
  • Various food journalism articles and fan databases

Some restaurants may have closed, changed names, or relocated since Bourdain's visits. Status information is best-effort and may not be current.
